Governed Archive

Archive

Archive is the governance layer for long-term file retention: pattern-first naming, independent logging, duplicate safety, and nested `.archive` flattening for the DOS estate.

The command stub exists first because the policy matters. This is not blind compression or accidental cleanup. It is a controlled archive pattern for files and folders that need to stay recoverable, auditable, and easy to reason about over time.

Open archive.cmd Back to repos

What Archive Does Pattern First

File And Folder Pattern

Archive applies a naming contract to both files and folders: filename_ext-YYYYMMDD-[Day of Week, 3 letters]-hhmmss.cs.FILES and filename_ext-YYYYMMDD-[Day of Week, 3 letters]-hhmmss.cs.FOLDERS.

Independent Log

The archive log stays separate so archive events do not get buried inside other cleanup or sweep activity.

Scope Control

The current scope is \DOS only. That keeps the blast radius low while the pattern and policy are refined.

Strengths Retention + Clarity

Recoverable Naming

Names stay deterministic, and dot characters inside the base name are preserved instead of being flattened away.

Duplicate Safety

Duplicates do not stop the archive. They get tagged so the process continues without losing provenance.

Nested .archive Flattening

If a folder under .archive contains its own .archive, the nested content gets moved up and the inner container is removed.

Policy Over Guessing

Files or folders that do not match the pattern get renamed rather than left ambiguous.

Weaknesses It Overcomes From Ad Hoc To Governed

Archive exists to remove the weakness of ad hoc retention: stray names, inconsistent folder history, duplicate collisions, and nested archive clutter.

Instead of treating long-term storage as a side effect, it treats it as a governed process with naming rules, logging, and repeatable behavior.

Why A Founder Cares Low Friction

A founder should care because retention problems become expensive when teams need to preserve evidence, history, or old system states and nobody can reliably find what happened.

Archive keeps the old material legible without turning the system into a manual sorting project.

Future Direction Not Yet Active

Processor Later

The current file is policy-only. The processor can come later once the rules are stable and the archive story is ready to execute.

Menu Surface

The next layer can become a menu or command surface that makes the archive rules easy to apply without exposing unnecessary complexity.

Source Note Policy Stub

archive.cmd is intentionally inert for now. It records the governance rules, keeps the policy visible, and leaves execution for later.

The current pattern is the governance for archived files.
